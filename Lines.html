Linear Relationships

Many relationships are approximately linear. If we graph the effect that the independent variable (the one I know/have/start with) has on the dependent variable (the one that changes as a result of changes in the independent variable), we put the independent variable on the x-axis and the dependent variable on the y-axis.

Form and Function

Lines are most commonly represented in the form y=mx+b

x: independent variable

y: dependent variable

m: slope

b: intercept

Slope and Intercept

The slope (m) is the slant or rate of change. It represents how much y will change if x is increase by 1. If m > 0, the line goes up and to the right. If m < 0, the line goes down and to the left. The intercept (b), is how much of y we have when x=0. Itâ€™s actually where the line cuts the y-axis, and is given by the point (0,b).
